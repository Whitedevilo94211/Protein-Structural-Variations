Creation of a Database for Protein Structural Variations in Cancer Mutations

Creating a database for protein structural variations in cancer mutations is a significant step in advancing cancer research and precision medicine. Such a database would compile detailed information about somatic mutations in cancer, focusing on how these mutations alter protein structures and their functional implications. It could include data on amino acid substitutions, clustering of mutations in 3D protein models, and their association with specific cancer types. Tools like COSMIC, CancerVar, and mutation3D already provide resources for studying somatic mutations and their structural impacts, offering insights into functional hotspots and aiding in hypothesis generation. This database would be invaluable for identifying potential therapeutic targets and understanding the molecular mechanisms driving cancer progression

This database provides critical information on protein structural variations in cancer mutations, including key genes and proteins involved,post-translational modifications (PTMs), cellular localization of proteins, and 3D structures.
